ABOUT THE PROJECT

Why Attend Eventa

38% of children in Holmes County, Mississippi, are reported as food insecure. This issue is crystal clear in Durant Elementary School (DES), part of the Holmes County Consolidated School District, where for many students the only meal of the day is in the school cafeteria. Feeding the Future Mississippi (FFMS) seeks to fill in the gap by working to ensure that no child in the Holmes County School System goes hungry.
Through a partnership with the Mississippi Food Network (a subsidiary of Feeding America), local business, and churches, FFMS provides a discreetly-filled backpack with nutritious meals to some of the most chronically food-insecure students in DES during weekends and short holidays throughout the school year. Each of these nonperishable packages includes two breakfast meals, two entrées, two 100% fruit juice boxes, two fruits, and one dairy item.
Each meal is freshly prepared and delivered to Durant Elementary School by the Mississippi Food Network, a certified 501(c)(3) nonprofit. The meals are then distributed by a school employee or a local FFMS volunteer. The operational costs are such that one child can be fed for one weekend during the school year for as little as $4, and for every weekend throughout the entire school year, a child can be fed for just $144.
During a pilot study throughout the 2018-2019 school year, 33 children, determined as the most chronically food insecure by the school district, were successfully fed for ten consecutive weeks. This organization aims to ensure that each of these 33 students, and hopefully many more, consistently receive weekend meals over the entire 2019-2020 school year.
